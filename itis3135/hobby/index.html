This hobby site is for video games!

So, what exactly are video games, for those who may not know?

Video games are digital games with stunning graphics and audio to captivate a player and draw them in, where a player can control a character within this digital space. Video games can range from simple farming simulators and puzzles to role-playing games and competitive online gaming. Video games are available on a large selection of platforms for any kind of player!

Who Plays Video Games?

Everyone is capable of playing video games!

Video games can be played by anyone of any age. Video games are designed for a wide range of age and skill groups. Some games are even created for specific demographics, such as children or older people. Video games for younger people often focus on a learning experience for mental development.

In recent years, video games have become more and more inclusive to those who may require more accessibility while playing. This accessibility can range from colorblind options, to subtitles, and other options for those who may be hearing impared.

When to Play and Managing Time

Heres how!

The best thing to do when it comes to managing your time while gaming is to set time limits for yourself. This can be done using an alarm or parental controls available within some games. Make sure to prioritize your responsibilities, and remember that gaming is a hobby!

Another tip for managing time would be to play games that have a pause function. If you have a busy schedule, it would be a good idea to stay away from online multiplayer games that lack a pause and save function.

How to Get Started in Playing Games?

Learn how here!

  1. Choose your platform.
    • First things first with video games, pick which platform works for you! Theres many different thing to consider like cost and availability. With computer games, physical versions are not as common as they used to be. So, if you're interested in collecting physical copies of your games, try sticking to console gaming!
  2. Pick a genre.
    • Picking a genre is a key aspect in gaming. Finding a genre may take some time, or could even be an instantaneous thing for some. Video games have a wide variety of genres for all kinds of players. Some popular genres are first-person shooters, role-playing games, simulators, and puzzle games!
  3. Pick up your controller or mouse and keyboard, and learn the controls.
    • For some, this may very well be the hardest step if you haven't been gaming from a younge age. It takes good hand eye coordination to focus on the screen and not what your hands are doing on the keyboard or controller. With practice, it will get easier!
  4. Focus on enjoyment, not winning.
    • A key detail in gaming is to focus on enjoying yourself if you choose to pursue it as a hobby. If you eventually decide to pursue competitive gaming, be sure to still try and enjoy casual gaming when you can!

Why should you play video games?

Video games (in moderation) can be good for you.

Over the years, many different studies have taken place to see if video games can help or hinder ones mental health and development. Studies show that video games can aid in cognitive performance in children. A repetitive task such as gaming has been shown to help with memory and even help children who experience attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder.

Another study shows that video games can be a good source of stress relief for those unable to seek such an outlet elsewhere. Many that play video games report that they help their mental health, and help them to feel happier in their day to day lives.
